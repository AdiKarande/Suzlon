India's Most
Trusted Winds

1995

Formation of Suzlon Energy Limited

India's first renewable energy company was born from one man’s conviction that progress and the planet can go together.

1996

First 0.27 MW wind turbine commissioned

Suzlon got its first order from PSU giant – IPCL, with the first wind turbine being installed at Dhank, Gujarat.

1997

First wind energy manufacturing facility

India’s Aatmanirbhar journey in wind began with Suzlon’s first turbine manufacturing facility at Daman, with only 800 MW annual capacity.

2000

First private sector project

India Inc. bought into the promise of wind energy when Suzlon won Tata Finance’s turnkey wind farm project to reduce its power costs and carbon footprint alike.

2001

First 1 MW wind turbine commissioned

Suzlon installed India’s first, game-changing megawatt-class wind turbine at Vankusawade, Satara, for Niskalp Investments, elevating the renewables game in India from kilowatts to megawatts.

2001

AE Rotor Holding BV, Netherlands, established

Suzlon established its first office on foreign soil with a rotor technology setup in the Netherlands, marking Suzlon’s endeavour to harness the best global minds.

2002

India made its first blades in India

Crafted locally in Daman, India’s first-ever wind turbine blades – Suzlon’s S60 marked a breakthrough in homegrown wind tech and manufacturing, paving the way for more jobs, local capacity, and a new wind economy.

2002 - 2005

Expanding across continents

With technology widely accepted, business models that were customer centric, and Indian ingenuity, Suzlon rapidly entered markets across continents, often featuring among the top 3 in each geography.

2003

Suzlon enters the U.S. market

India's first wind energy deliveries to the U.S. started with Suzlon’s first order there. It also flagged off the journey of Indian wind energy exports to the world – the start of a global company in the making.

2003

Asia's largest wind farm in Maharashtra

Suzlon set global benchmarks by building Asia’s largest wind farm at Vankusawade, Maharashtra – going from single turbines to utility scale windfarms.

2004

Introducing China to wind manufacturing

Suzlon established China’s first wind energy manufacturing facility in Tianjin at a time when the country only imported wind turbines, bringing affordable clean energy solutions to millions.

2005

India’s first-ever renewable energy IPO

Suzlon brought wind to every Indian through its public listing. The ‘IPO of the year’ was oversubscribed by 15x on Day 1.

2005

Suzlon ranked 9th globally by BTM Report

Suzlon became the first and only Indian wind company to enter the global top ten, proving homegrown wind technology could compete and lead worldwide.

2005

First 2 MW turbine commissioned

Suzlon dedicated India's largest turbine at the time to the nation on Independence Day, symbolising freedom from fossil fuels and a new kind of power.

2005

1,000 MW milestone crossed

What began as a cottage industry matured into an established sector with Suzlon’s 1,000 MW milestone, marking a new phase of a self-reliant India.

2006

Hansen Transmissions acquired

In India’s second-largest foreign acquisition, Belgium’s Hansen Transmissions became part of the Suzlon family, solving a global gearbox shortage.

2006

Ranked fifth WTG manufacturer globally

As Suzlon grew from strength to strength, India, too, rose in global recognition, proving that world-class wind technology could be imagined, built, and exported from India.

2007

SE Forge Limited formed

Suzlon established India’s first dedicated foundries for wind energy at Coimbatore and Vadodara, taking another step in ‘self-reliance’.

2007

Asia's first blade testing facility established

India became the only country after the Netherlands and the USA to qualify and certify wind tech as per international standards.

2007

First wind energy company to receive FDI

A 40 MW project from British Petroleum made Suzlon the only Indian manufacturer to attract Foreign Direct Investment into the wind energy sector.

2008

Harvard Business School case study

Harvard Business School wrote “The Suzlon Edge,” a case study that analysed Suzlon’s bold strategies and rapid rise.

2009

Suzlon and TERI University sign an MoU

Suzlon introduced the very first M. Tech in Renewable Energy to nurture young talent into innovators for the nation’s tomorrow.

2010

Ranked third WTG manufacturer globally

Suzlon became the first and only Indian wind energy company to reach the global top three, proving that scale, technology, and vision can rise from Indian soil.

2010

Among the world’s greenest HQ

The nation became home to one of the world’s greenest corporate campuses, Suzlon One Earth in Pune, a physical form of Suzlon's sustainable vision.

2014

Asia's largest wind park in Gujarat

At 1,100 MW, the Kutch Wind Park emerged as one of India’s largest symbol of hope and scale in the fight against climate change.

2014

India’s first Hybrid Lattice Tower introduced

At 120 metres, Suzlon’s hybrid lattice–tubular tower at Jamanwada reached higher to tap stronger winds, boost output, and expand wind’s reach.

2017

11 GW milestone achieved

More wind energy for more cities, industries, and homes, improving lives and strengthening India's clean energy leadership.

2018

Suzlon’s largest WTG, S128, commissioned

At 140m hub height, Suzlon launched ️India's largest wind turbine generator ever at the time, redefining efficiency in clean energy generation.

2020

Restructuring for growth

Suzlon restructured its debt and business models, and combined with a ₹1,200 crore Rights Issue, it enabled the company to take on the future from a position of strength.

2022-23

Debt-free and positioned to lead

Investors rallied behind Suzlon’s turnaround, which made Suzlon net debt-free, unlocking greater value for stakeholders.

2023

20 GW installed worldwide

As Suzlon became the only Indian company to install over 20 GW across 17 countries, Indian wind energy made its mark with global trust and respect.

2023

S144, Suzlon’s 3 MW platform launched

A new generation of turbines, built for India's diverse weather conditions, delivers more low-cost power and care to nature.

2024

Safeguarding India’s renewable assets

Committed to protecting the nation's smaller wind energy assets, Suzlon acquired India’s largest multi-brand OMS provider, Renom Energy Services.

2025

Best Year in a Decade

Suzlon’s transformation led to its best performance in 10 years, with a record 5.6 GW order book, stronger deliveries, and record-high revenue and profits.
